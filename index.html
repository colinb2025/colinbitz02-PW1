About Me

I am a current second-year student-athlete at the University of Virginia. I will graduate with a major in Computer Science and a minor in Data Science. I enjoy working with all fields of math and science as well as interest in working with data. I find joy in creating projects and software that will help those around me.

I have profound experience within a team structure, having served as a leader as well a follower. After unanimously being selected as captain of my high school team and serving as an advocate for the University of Virginia swim team in my first year, I have become comfortable in a leadership position. Additionally, I have been an employee under several companies and I have found joy in following in the footsteps of peers and finding a way to impress those around me while contributing to a team goal.

Colin Bitz: Projects

Throughout my several years in computer science, I have found that there are certain topics which stand out to me as something I'd love to learn more about. I have grown very fond of pathfinding and sorting algorithms, linked lists, and creating functional software to benefit others. In pursuit of developing a deeper understanding on these topics, I have created software relating to my points of interest. These projects include a weather app, a to-do list, a linked list visualizer, and an interactive pathfinding algorithm. Below, you will be able to find more information about each project, skills used in the project, and a link to the live product and github. Enjoy!

Linked List Visualizer

After taking Data Structures and Algorithms, I was particularly fascinated with and successful with using Linked Lists. I was aware that a large population of my class struggled with the concepts behind linked lists so I wanted to make something to visualize the several functions that you can use on a linked list. In this project, you will find functions including Insert/Remove at Tail/Head, Insert at Index, Find, Get, as well as the ability to manipulate and change the linked list.

Study Games Website

Quizlet, a famous website known for creating games built around studying, created a platform to make studying more enjoyable. That being said, I, along with one friend, created a new website to make studying enjoyable once again. In the process, we created new games such as a kahoot-inspired mode in addition to several other new game modes.
